Hey there! I'm Daniel, a physicist turned data scientist with a passion for using technology to solve real-world problems. My journey has taken me from studying quantum mechanics in dimly lit physics labs to building machine learning models that help improve healthcare delivery in underserved communities.

When I'm not diving into datasets or debugging code, you'll find me on mountain trails, either running or biking through nature. There's something about the rhythm of footsteps on dirt paths and the challenge of steep climbs that clears my mind and sparks creativity. Some of my best problem-solving happens at 10,000 feet!

I'm fascinated by the intersection of AI and global health, believing that thoughtful application of technology can dramatically improve lives worldwide. My work focuses on making these advanced tools accessible and practical, whether that's predicting disease outbreaks or optimizing resource allocation in challenging environments.

Beyond the technical side, I'm an avid reader who believes that the best insights often come from unexpected connections‚Äîwhether that's between a physics equation and a biological system, or between a trail run and a coding challenge. I write about these connections and my technical work on Medium, where I try to make complex topics accessible to curious minds.
